Post-Menopausal Bleeding

A Comprehensive Overview

What is Post-Menopausal Bleeding?

  • Any vaginal bleeding that occurs after menopause
  • Menopause: 12 consecutive months without menstruation
  • Always requires medical evaluation

Causes

  • Endometrial atrophy
  • Endometrial hyperplasia
  • Endometrial cancer
  • Polyps
  • Hormone Replacement Therapy
  • Other causes (e.g., cervical cancer, vaginal atrophy)

Risk Factors

  • Obesity
  • Diabetes
  • Hypertension
  • Use of tamoxifen
  • Late menopause (after age 55)

Diagnostic Approach

  1. Detailed medical history
  2. Physical examination
  3. Transvaginal ultrasound
  4. Endometrial biopsy
  5. Hysteroscopy (if necessary)

Treatment

  • Depends on the underlying cause
  • May include:
    • Medication
    • Surgical procedures
    • Lifestyle changes

When to Seek Medical Help

  • Any vaginal bleeding after menopause
  • Even spotting should be evaluated
  • Early detection is crucial for best outcomes

Conclusion

  • Post-menopausal bleeding is not normal
  • Various causes, some serious
  • Prompt medical evaluation is essential
  • Most cases are treatable when caught early
