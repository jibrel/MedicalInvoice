Comparison of ICP Guidelines:
2011 vs 2022

1. Introduction

This presentation compares the old (2011) and new (2022) guidelines on Intrahepatic Cholestasis of Pregnancy (ICP), including relevant statistics and findings.

2. Prevalence

Old (2011):

  • 0.7% in multi-ethnic populations in England
  • 1.2-1.5% in women of Indian-Asian or Pakistani-Asian origin

2. Prevalence

New (2022):

  • Maintains the same prevalence statistics

Similarity:

Both guidelines acknowledge variation in prevalence among different populations

3. Diagnostic Criteria

Old (2011):

  • Pruritus in pregnancy
  • Abnormal liver function tests (LFTs) beyond pregnancy-specific limits
  • No specific bile acid threshold mentioned

3. Diagnostic Criteria

New (2022):

  • Pruritus in pregnancy
  • Raised peak random total bile acid concentration of ≥19 μmol/L
  • Normal pregnancy-specific upper limit for bile acids: 18 μmol/L

Key Difference:

New guideline emphasizes specific bile acid threshold for diagnosis

4. Risk Stratification

Old (2011):

Did not provide clear risk stratification based on bile acid levels

4. Risk Stratification

New (2022):

Categorizes ICP based on peak bile acid levels:

  • Mild: 19-39 μmol/L
  • Moderate: 40-99 μmol/L
  • Severe: ≥100 μmol/L

4. Risk Stratification

Stillbirth Risk (New guideline):

  • Mild ICP: 0.13% (similar to background risk)
  • Moderate ICP: 0.28%
  • Severe ICP: 3.44%
  • UK national stillbirth rate (2015): 0.29%

5. Fetal Monitoring

Old (2011):

  • Suggested various monitoring techniques (e.g., CTG, ultrasound)
  • Acknowledged limitations in predicting fetal death

5. Fetal Monitoring

New (2022):

  • States that ultrasound and CTG do not predict or prevent stillbirth
  • Emphasizes maternal monitoring of fetal movements

Key Change:

New guideline is more explicit about limitations of fetal monitoring in preventing adverse outcomes

6. Associated Risks

Preterm Birth (New guideline):

  • Mild ICP: 16.5%
  • Moderate ICP: 19.1%
  • Severe ICP: 30.5%

6. Associated Risks

Other Risks (New guideline):

  • Pre-eclampsia: OR 3.7 (95% CI 3.2-4.3)
  • Gestational diabetes: OR 2.4 (95% CI 2.1-2.8)
  • Meconium-stained amniotic fluid: OR 2.60 (95% CI 1.62-4.16)

Old guideline did not provide such detailed risk statistics

7. Treatment - Ursodeoxycholic Acid (UDCA)

Old (2011):

  • Suggested UDCA might be beneficial
  • Limited evidence from small studies

7. Treatment - Ursodeoxycholic Acid (UDCA)

New (2022):

  • Does not routinely recommend UDCA for improving perinatal outcomes
  • Based on larger randomized controlled trials
  • May reduce maternal itch by about 5mm on a 100mm scale
  • May reduce spontaneous preterm birth <37 weeks (aOR 0.46, 95% CI 0.25-0.86)

8. Timing of Delivery

Old (2011):

  • Suggested considering delivery around 37-38 weeks
  • No clear stratification based on severity

8. Timing of Delivery

New (2022):

  • Mild ICP: Consider planned birth by 40 weeks
  • Moderate ICP: Consider planned birth at 38-39 weeks
  • Severe ICP: Consider planned birth at 35-36 weeks

Key Change:

New guideline provides more nuanced recommendations based on severity

9. Long-term Maternal Health

New (2022) guideline includes long-term health data:

  • Increased risk of hepatobiliary disease: HR 2.62 (95% CI 2.47-2.77)
  • Increased risk of gallstone disease: HR 2.72 (95% CI 2.55-2.91)
  • Increased risk of immune-mediated diseases: HR 1.28 (95% CI 1.19-1.38)

Old guideline did not include long-term follow-up data

10. Conclusion

The 2022 guideline reflects significant advancements in our understanding of ICP:

  • More precise diagnostic criteria with specific bile acid thresholds
  • Clearer risk stratification based on bile acid levels
  • More nuanced management strategies, especially regarding timing of delivery
  • Greater caution in interventions without clear evidence of benefit (e.g., UDCA, fetal monitoring)
  • Inclusion of long-term maternal health data

These changes aim to improve care for women with ICP while providing more personalized management based on disease severity.
